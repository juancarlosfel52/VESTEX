# Company Pattern Signal Interpretation Guide
## What Each Event Means for Stock Price: 1 Day / 1 Week / 1 Month

---

## HOW TO READ THIS GUIDE

- All returns are AVERAGE historical reactions from large-cap and mid-cap US equities (2000-2023)
- Individual stock reactions vary widely; treat these as probabilistic ranges, not certainties
- Context always matters — a beat in a bear market matters less than a beat in a bull market
- Confidence ratings are based on volume of academic/empirical evidence

---

## EARNINGS EVENTS

### Earnings Beat (Small, < 5% above consensus)
- **1 Day**: +0.5% to +1.5%
- **1 Week**: +0.5% to +2.0% (drift begins)
- **1 Month**: +1.0% to +3.0% (PEAD continuation)
- **Interpretation**: Modest positive. Market already expected a beat — the question is magnitude. Small beats often produce muted reactions or even slight sell-offs in overvalued stocks.
- **Confidence**: HIGH (robust across studies)

### Earnings Beat (Large, > 10% above consensus)
- **1 Day**: +3.0% to +6.0%
- **1 Week**: +4.0% to +8.0%
- **1 Month**: +5.0% to +10.0%
- **Interpretation**: Strong positive. Large surprises generate meaningful post-earnings drift. Watch for high short interest — a large beat with heavy short interest can trigger a squeeze.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Earnings Miss (Small, < 5% below consensus)
- **1 Day**: -2.0% to -4.0%
- **1 Week**: -2.5% to -5.0%
- **1 Month**: -4.0% to -8.0%
- **Interpretation**: Negative. Misses are punished ~2.5x harder than beats are rewarded (asymmetry). Even small misses create downward drift. Check if miss is due to revenue weakness or margin compression — revenue weakness is worse.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Earnings Miss (Large, > 10% below consensus)
- **1 Day**: -7.0% to -15.0%
- **1 Week**: -8.0% to -18.0%
- **1 Month**: -10.0% to -22.0%
- **Interpretation**: Very negative. Large misses often reflect structural problems. Earnings estimate cuts follow, triggering further institutional selling. If guidance is also cut (double miss), use the more extreme range.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Double Miss (Miss + Guidance Cut)
- **1 Day**: -10.0% to -25.0%
- **1 Week**: -12.0% to -28.0%
- **1 Month**: -15.0% to -35.0%
- **Interpretation**: Severe negative. The most bearish single-event combination in earnings season. Analyst price targets will be cut, institutional holders will reduce positions over subsequent weeks. This is when stocks "fall off a cliff."
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Double Beat (Beat + Guidance Raise)
- **1 Day**: +3.0% to +8.0%
- **1 Week**: +4.0% to +10.0%
- **1 Month**: +6.0% to +15.0%
- **Interpretation**: Very bullish. Most powerful single-quarter signal. Analyst estimate revisions follow, driving institutional buying. In momentum-favored market environments, these stocks become "must-own" for growth funds.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

## GUIDANCE EVENTS

### Guidance Raise (Mid-Quarter, not at earnings)
- **1 Day**: +2.0% to +5.0%
- **1 Week**: +2.5% to +6.0%
- **1 Month**: +3.0% to +8.0%
- **Interpretation**: Bullish. Pre-announced guidance raises signal management confidence. Analyst upgrades typically follow within 1-2 weeks.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM-HIGH

### Guidance Cut (Profit Warning / Pre-Announcement)
- **1 Day**: -8.0% to -18.0%
- **1 Week**: -9.0% to -20.0%
- **1 Month**: -10.0% to -25.0%
- **Interpretation**: Bearish. Among the most negative single-event signals. When a company warns before earnings, it signals that visibility was poor enough that management couldn't wait for the quarterly report. A second guidance cut within 2 quarters is a severe red flag.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

## CORPORATE ACTION EVENTS

### Layoff Announcement (Efficiency / Cost-Cut Context)
- **1 Day**: +1.5% to +5.0%
- **1 Week**: +1.0% to +4.0%
- **1 Month**: Depends entirely on margin improvement confirmation
- **Interpretation**: Conditionally positive. The market rewards proof of cost discipline. Key question: is the layoff LEADING (proactive efficiency) or LAGGING (reactive to revenue decline)? Check: did they maintain revenue guidance? If yes, view as bullish.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM

### Layoff Announcement (Distress / Revenue Decline Context)
- **1 Day**: -3.0% to -8.0%
- **1 Week**: -4.0% to -10.0%
- **1 Month**: -8.0% to -20.0%
- **Interpretation**: Negative. When layoffs accompany revenue guidance cuts, the market correctly interprets them as a sign of deteriorating business fundamentals. Institutional holders reduce exposure.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM-HIGH

### Acquisition Announced (Acquirer / Buyer)
- **1 Day**: -1.5% to -4.0%
- **1 Week**: -1.0% to -3.0%
- **1 Month**: Flat to -2% (if deal is accretive, can recover)
- **Interpretation**: Usually mildly negative for acquirer. Market fears overpayment, integration risk, and dilution (if stock deal). Cash deals are viewed more favorably. Large strategic acquisitions that fill an obvious gap (e.g., Meta buying Instagram) are better received long-term.
- **Confidence**: HIGH (most studied M&A literature)

### Acquisition Announced (Target / Seller)
- **1 Day**: +20% to +35%
- **1 Week**: Holds near deal price
- **1 Month**: Holds near deal price (with merger arb premium removed over time)
- **Interpretation**: Strongly bullish for target — this is the most reliable single-event return in public markets. Risk: deal falls through (returns to pre-deal price minus risk premium).
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Buyback Announcement
- **1 Day**: +1.5% to +3.0%
- **1 Week**: +1.0% to +2.5%
- **1 Month**: +2.0% to +5.0% (if executed)
- **Interpretation**: Positive signal of management confidence in undervaluation. Strongest when: FCF is strong, debt is low, stock is near historical P/E lows. Weakest when: debt-funded buyback or buyback used to offset stock-based compensation.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM-HIGH

## INSIDER TRANSACTIONS

### Insider Cluster Buy (3+ insiders buying open market)
- **1 Day**: +0.5% to +1.5% (immediate reaction small)
- **1 Week**: +1.0% to +3.0%
- **1 Month**: +5.0% to +12.0%
- **Interpretation**: Strong bullish signal over medium term. Insiders rarely buy simultaneously unless they see significant undervaluation or expect a positive catalyst. The signal is strongest in small/mid-cap stocks where insiders have most direct visibility.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Single Insider Buy (CEO or CFO, large size)
- **1 Day**: +0.5% to +1.0%
- **1 Week**: +1.0% to +3.0%
- **1 Month**: +3.0% to +8.0%
- **Interpretation**: Positive but verify it's an open-market purchase (Form 4 Transaction Code "P"), not an option exercise. CEO purchases are more meaningful than director purchases.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM-HIGH

### Insider Cluster Sell (3+ insiders selling, unscheduled)
- **1 Day**: -0.5% to -1.5%
- **1 Week**: -1.0% to -3.0%
- **1 Month**: -2.0% to -5.0%
- **Interpretation**: Mildly bearish but requires context. Insiders sell for many reasons. Most concerning when: unscheduled, large percentage of holdings, and CEO/CFO are both selling simultaneously.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM (lower than buying signal)

## SHORT INTEREST SIGNALS

### High Short Interest (>20% of float) with Rising Price
- **1 Day**: +2% to +10% (squeeze ignition day)
- **1 Week**: +10% to +50% (active squeeze)
- **1 Month**: Highly variable — squeezes reverse violently
- **Interpretation**: Short squeeze risk. Not a fundamental buy signal — these moves are tactical and driven by mechanics. Most short squeezes fully retrace within 1-3 months as short sellers rebuild positions or fundamentals reassert.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM (timing unpredictable)

### High Short Interest (>20% of float) Declining Stock
- **1 Day**: -1% to -3%
- **1 Week**: -3% to -8%
- **1 Month**: -10% to -25%
- **Interpretation**: Bears are right. High short interest + declining price = confirmation of short thesis. Increasing short interest in a downtrend accelerates the decline.
- **Confidence**: HIGH (this is the base case for high short interest)

## DEBT / BALANCE SHEET EVENTS

### Sudden Debt Increase (>20% of total debt)
- **1 Day**: -1% to -3% (if announced)
- **1 Week**: -1% to -4%
- **1 Month**: Depends on use of proceeds
- **Interpretation**: Negative if funded for unclear purposes or in a rising rate environment. Neutral/positive if funding a clearly accretive acquisition or strong growth capex.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM

### Credit Rating Downgrade
- **1 Day**: -2% to -6%
- **1 Week**: -3% to -8%
- **1 Month**: -5% to -15%
- **Interpretation**: Significant negative event. Forces institutional bond holders to sell (many can't hold sub-investment-grade). Triggers covenant concerns. Often a lagging indicator but creates forced selling regardless.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Debt-to-Equity Exceeds 3.0
- Not a single event, but a threshold crossing
- **Typical response**: Gradual multiple compression of 10-20% over 6-12 months
- **Interpretation**: Long-term bearish. High leverage amplifies any earnings volatility. Any recession or rate increase creates disproportionate pain.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

## FILING-BASED SIGNALS

### Going Concern Audit Opinion (10-K)
- **Immediate reaction**: -15% to -40%
- **3-Month outlook**: -30% to -70% (many of these companies eventually delist)
- **Interpretation**: Extreme negative. Auditors only issue going concern when bankruptcy risk is material. This is the strongest negative filing signal.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Financial Restatement
- **Day of announcement**: -5% to -25%
- **1 Month**: -15% to -40%
- **Interpretation**: Very negative. Signals accounting controls are broken and historical earnings were unreliable. Often triggers SEC investigation and class-action lawsuits.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

### Late Filing (NT 10-K or NT 10-Q)
- **Day of announcement**: -3% to -12%
- **1 Month**: -8% to -20%
- **Interpretation**: Moderate to strong negative. Usually precedes a restatement, material weakness disclosure, or auditor dispute. The uncertainty premium is punished immediately.
- **Confidence**: HIGH

---

## SIGNAL STACKING RULE
When multiple signals occur simultaneously, effects are roughly additive up to a practical ceiling:
- Double positive (e.g., large beat + guidance raise + cluster insider buy): cumulative 1-month return can reach +15% to +25%
- Double negative (e.g., guidance cut + credit downgrade + insider selling): cumulative 1-month return can reach -25% to -45%
- Contradictory signals (e.g., beat but guidance cut): guidance cut dominates; day 1 reaction typically negative despite beat
