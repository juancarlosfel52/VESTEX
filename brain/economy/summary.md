## THE MACRO MACHINE: HOW IT ALL CONNECTS

```
GDP GROWTH ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    ↑ driven by consumer spending (70%), investment, govt         │
    ↓ contracts when credit tightens or confidence falls          │
                                                                   ▼
EMPLOYMENT ←──────────────────── CONSUMER SPENDING           RECESSIONS
    ↑ jobs → wages → spending                ↑ PCE = 70% GDP
    ↓ layoffs → fear → saving              ↓ retail weak
          ↑                                        ↓
     INFLATION ←──────── TIGHT LABOR ────────────→ FED RESPONSE
          ↑                                        ↓
     INTEREST RATES ←───── FED TIGHTENS ────────────
          ↑                                        ↓
     YIELD CURVE ←──────── SHORT RATES RISE ───────→ INVERSION
          ↑                                        ↓
     CREDIT SPREADS ←───── TIGHTER CREDIT ─────────→ WIDEN
```

| Indicator | Rising = | Falling = | Market Impact |
|-----------|----------|-----------|---------------|
| CPI/PCE | Inflationary pressure → Fed tightens | Disinflationary → Fed eases | Rising bad for stocks short-term; falling good |
| GDP Growth | Economic expansion | Contraction/Recession | Strong GDP = bull environment |
| Unemployment | Labor loosening → deflation risk | Tight labor → wage inflation | Rising = bearish; falling = mixed (watch wages) |

| Consumer Confidence | Spending ahead | Spending pullback | Leading indicator for GDP |
| Initial Jobless Claims | Labor weakening | Labor strong | Spike >400K = recession alarm |

## THE ECONOMIC CYCLE AND WHAT TO EXPECT IN MARKETS

### EARLY EXPANSION (post-recession trough)
- GDP turning positive after contraction
- Unemployment still high but claims falling
- Fed at zero or cutting
- Yield curve steep (short rates low, long rates rising with growth)
- Credit spreads tightening from wide levels
- Best sectors: Cyclicals (discretionary, materials, industrials), small caps
- Worst: Defensives (utilities, staples)
- Equity signal: Strong bull market typically begins here (most gain is in first 12 months)

### MID EXPANSION
- GDP growing 2.5–4%
- Unemployment below NAIRU, wages rising
- Fed neutral to slightly tightening
- Yield curve normal, slight flattening
- Credit spreads near cycle lows
- Best sectors: Technology, financials, energy
- Equity signal: Bull market continues, moderate returns

### LATE EXPANSION (cycle peak approaching)
- GDP growth decelerating
- Unemployment near multi-decade lows
- Fed tightening aggressively
- Yield curve flattening or inverting
- Credit spreads bottomed and starting to widen
- Consumer confidence at peak
- Best sectors: Energy, materials, defensive rotation begins
- Equity signal: Volatility increases; risk/reward deteriorating

### RECESSION
- GDP negative for 2+ quarters; NBER confirms
- Unemployment rising (Sahm Rule triggered)
- Fed pivoting to cuts
- Yield curve steep again (bear steepener → bull steepener)
- Credit spreads wide and often still widening
- Consumer sentiment at lows
- Best assets: Long duration Treasuries, gold, cash, defensive equities
- Equity signal: Max drawdown period; average S&P decline ~32%

### RECOVERY ONSET
- GDP turning positive, still below trend
- Unemployment still rising (lags GDP by 6–12 months)
- Fed cutting aggressively or at floor
- Yield curve steep
- Spreads starting to narrow from peaks
- Equity signal: Strongest forward returns begin, even though news is still terrible

## INFLATION SIGNAL INTERPRETATION

| PCE Reading | Signal | Market Implication |
|-------------|--------|--------------------|
| < 1.5% | Deflation risk | Fed eases; bonds rally; equity depends on cause |
| 1.5–2.5% | Target zone | Goldilocks; best equity environment |
| 2.5–4.0% | Above target | Fed watches; rate hike risks; slight equity headwind |
| 4.0–6.0% | Hot inflation | Aggressive tightening; P/E compression; bonds sell |
| 6.0–9.0% | Severe inflation | Rapid hike cycle; equity bear market likely |
| >9.0% | Crisis inflation | Extreme tightening; stagflation risk; gold/commodities |

- Core PCE is the Fed trigger — if it rises 3 consecutive months, expect hawkish pivot
- Breakeven inflation (5Y5Y forward, FRED: T5YIFR) shows market inflation expectations

## UNEMPLOYMENT SIGNAL INTERPRETATION

| U-3 Level | Context | Market Signal |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| < 3.5% | Extreme tightness | Wage inflation; Fed tightens more |
| 3.5–4.5% | Healthy, at/below NAIRU | Neutral; watch wage growth |
| 4.5–5.5% | Near natural rate | Balanced; minimal inflationary pressure |
| 5.5–7.0% | Above natural rate | Labor slack; disinflation; Fed may ease |
| 7.0–10% | Recession territory | Aggressive easing; equity bear market ongoing |
| > 10% | Severe recession | Crisis response; fiscal + monetary stimulus |

- Sahm Rule: When triggered (3M avg +0.5pp above 12M low) → recession in progress
- Weekly initial claims: Best real-time signal. Trend above 350K = caution; above 400K = alarm

## YIELD CURVE SIGNAL INTERPRETATION

| 10Y-2Y Spread | Signal | Time to Act |
|---------------|--------|-------------|
| > +150bp | Steep: Early/mid expansion | Full risk-on |
| +50 to +150bp | Normal: Mid expansion | Stay long equities |
| 0 to +50bp | Flattening: Late cycle | Begin defensives, reduce duration risk |
| 0 to −50bp | Shallow inversion: Warning | Reduce risk, build cash |
| −50 to −100bp | Moderate inversion: Strong warning | Defensive positioning |
| < −100bp | Deep inversion: High recession probability | Maximum caution |

- Average time from first inversion to recession: 12–18 months
- Average time from first inversion to market peak: ~9–12 months
- After re-steepening from inversion: Market often falls (recession arriving)

## CREDIT SPREAD SIGNAL INTERPRETATION

| HY OAS | Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| < 300bp | Extremely tight, complacency | Risk-on max; watch for reversal |
| 300–450bp | Normal benign | Neutral |
| 450–600bp | Stress building | Reduce HY; defensive tilt |
| 600–800bp | Significant stress | Risk-off; add Treasuries |
| 800–1200bp | Crisis/recession | Maximum caution |
| > 1200bp | Systemic event | Forced selling, liquidity crisis |

- HY spreads lead equities by ~1–3 months at turning points
- Watch IG spreads too: IG widening >200bp is systemic risk signal

## THE "DASHBOARD" — RECESSION PROBABILITY FRAMEWORK

Score each indicator from 0 (safe) to 2 (danger):
1. Yield Curve (10Y-2Y): >+50bp=0, 0 to +50=1, negative=2
2. Sahm Rule: Not triggered=0, near trigger=1, triggered=2
3. ISM PMI: >55=0, 50–55=1, <50=2
4. HY Spreads: <400=0, 400–600=1, >600=2
5. Initial Claims trend: Falling=0, flat=1, rising=2
6. Conference Board LEI: Rising=0, flat=1, falling 6mo=2
7. Consumer Confidence: >100=0, 80–100=1, <80=2
8. PCE/CPI trend: Falling=0, stable=1, rising fast=2

Total score: 0–4 = Low risk | 5–9 = Moderate | 10–16 = High recession risk

## HISTORICAL PERFORMANCE BY MACRO REGIME

### Equities (S&P 500 avg annual return)
- Expansion + low inflation (2%): ~14% avg annual return
- Expansion + high inflation (>4%): ~5% avg real return
- Recession: avg −32% peak-to-trough
- Recovery (first 12 months from trough): avg +45%

### Bonds (10-Year Treasury)
- Fed tightening cycle: Negative return (prices fall as yields rise)
- Fed easing cycle: Strong positive returns (duration rally)
- Recession onset: Bonds often rally sharply (flight to quality)

### Gold
- Best in: Negative real rates + high inflation + crisis
- Worst in: Rising real rates + strong dollar
- Average during recessions: +10–15%

### Commodities
- Best in: Late expansion + supply shock + weak dollar
- Worst in: Recession (demand destruction)
