# News Intelligence Playbook — Tactical Reaction Guide
# Category 6: What to Do When Specific News Hits
# Last Updated: 2026-06-03

---

## HOW TO USE THIS PLAYBOOK

This playbook describes the TYPICAL market reaction to each news trigger across four time windows:
- **1H:** First hour after news breaks
- **1D:** End of day / closing price vs. pre-news
- **1W:** 5 trading days later
- **1M:** ~21 trading days later

**FADE THE REACTION** = trade against the initial move (bet on reversal)
**FOLLOW THE REACTION** = trade with the initial move (bet on continuation)

The single most important variable: **How much was already priced in before the news?**
A 10% EPS beat on a stock that rallied 30% into earnings is less bullish than the same beat on a stock that sold off going in. Always assess the pre-news setup before applying these templates.

## PLAYBOOK ENTRIES

---

### RATE CUT (FIRST IN CYCLE)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Flat to +0.5% (usually priced in before announcement)
- 1D: +0.5% to +1.5% if clean dovish cut; flat to negative if "hawkish cut" with fewer future cuts signaled
- 1W: +1% to +3%; sector rotation begins into rate-sensitives (REITs, utilities, small-caps)
- 1M: +3% to +6% if no recession signs; negative if cut is in response to economic deterioration

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Fed signals this is a one-time "insurance cut" and future cuts are uncertain
- Unemployment data is still deteriorating (cut is chasing recession, not preventing one)
- Yield curve does not steepen post-cut (bond market not believing the growth narrative)

**Follow the reaction when:**
- Economy is clearly soft landing; employment still strong; cut is purely accommodation removal
- Fed signals multiple future cuts and language is clearly dovish
- Historical pattern: non-recession first cut → 12-month return median +11.9%; follow the initial move and hold

**Key sectors to rotate into on confirmed easing cycle:**
Small-caps, REITs, regional banks (steeper yield curve), rate-sensitive growth

---

### RATE HIKE (FIRST IN CYCLE)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Volatile; initial selloff of -0.5% to -1.5%
- 1D: Flat to -1%; initial uncertainty resolves; "remove the uncertainty" rally possible
- 1W: -1% to -3%; market digesting new policy direction
- 1M: -3% to -7% (since 1999, S&P 500 always down 3 months after first hike on average)

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Economy is clearly strong (GDP >3%, unemployment <4%); hike is normalization not tightening
- Earnings season is active with strong beats; earnings power overrides rate concern
- 3-month pullback has historically been a buying opportunity; 12-month return positive

**Follow the reaction when:**
- Inflation is significantly above target AND Fed signals aggressive pace of hikes ahead
- Yield curve is already flat/inverted (signals market pricing in policy mistake)
- Financial conditions already tightening before Fed acts

**Key sectors to rotate into on rate hike cycle:**
Financials (early hikes benefit NIM), Energy, Materials; rotate out of high-multiple growth, REITs, Utilities

### EARNINGS BEAT — LARGE (EPS >10% ABOVE CONSENSUS)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Gap up +2% to +5% at open; first 30 minutes volatile as algos absorb the data
- 1D: +3% to +5% for typical large-cap; +8% to +15% for high-vol/small-cap names
- 1W: +3% to +6% (PEAD begins; analysts revise estimates upward)
- 1M: +5% to +10% (full PEAD drift over 60 trading days)

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Stock was up 20%+ in the 30 days before earnings (priced in; sell-the-news risk)
- Beat was from cost cuts, tax benefits, or one-time items (not revenue/organic growth)
- Guidance was merely maintained, not raised (beat but no acceleration narrative)
- P/E ratio is already at 40x+ and beat was 10-15% (valuation ceiling even with beat)

**Follow the reaction when:**
- Beat + Raised Guidance (strongest possible signal; PEAD works best here)
- Revenue also beat (organic growth, not financial engineering)
- Stock was flat or down going into earnings (no pre-pricing of beat)
- Analyst estimates likely lagging; forward estimates need significant revision

---

### EARNINGS MISS — LARGE (EPS >10% BELOW CONSENSUS)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Gap down -4% to -8% at open; sellers dominate first 30 minutes
- 1D: -5% to -8% for typical large-cap; -10% to -25% for high-vol/small-cap names
- 1W: -6% to -10% (negative PEAD begins; analyst downgrades follow)
- 1M: -8% to -15% (full negative PEAD drift; estimate resets continue)

**Fade the reaction when (rare):**
- Miss was clearly one-time/non-recurring (plant fire, weather event, clearly disclosed)
- Guidance was raised despite the miss (management confidence in trajectory)
- Stock had already sold off 30-40% ahead of earnings (miss was priced in; relief rally possible)
- Sector-wide macro issue (whole sector missing; no company-specific problems)

**Follow the reaction when:**
- Miss + Guidance cut (double negative; most reliably bad setup)
- Structural issue identified: competitive share loss, pricing pressure, obsolete product
- CEO/CFO language is cautious or vague about recovery timeline
- Analyst consensus was already conservative and stock still missed

---

### GUIDANCE RAISED

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Strong positive; +3% to +7% gap; market's forward-looking mechanism activates
- 1D: +4% to +8%; sustained buying throughout the session
- 1W: +5% to +10%; PEAD adds to gains as analysts revise estimates up
- 1M: +7% to +12%; most reliable positive catalyst after an earnings announcement

**When this fails:**
- Guidance raise is small (<1-2%) and market was expecting more
- Raised guidance still implies deceleration from current growth rate
- Valuation was already implying even higher guidance (DCF model already baked it in)

---

### GUIDANCE CUT

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Severe selloff; -5% to -15%; market hates forward visibility deterioration
- 1D: -6% to -15%; can be worse if CEO language is evasive or concerning
- 1W: -8% to -18%; analysts scramble to downgrade; new lower target prices published
- 1M: -10% to -25%; full negative PEAD; institutional unwinding takes weeks

**When this can be a buy:**
- Pre-announcement (company guides down before quarter ends): stock often sells off on pre-announcement, then trades flat-to-up when actual earnings confirm the pre-guide was conservative
- "Kitchen sink" quarter: management deliberately guides extremely low to reset expectations; next quarter very likely to beat
- New CEO/CFO taking writedowns: often a buying opportunity after the initial shock

### LAYOFFS — LARGE (5,000+ EMPLOYEES)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Mixed; technology/restructuring context → slight positive; recession/demand context → negative
- 1D: -1% to -3% average across all types; tech restructuring can be +1% to +3%
- 1W: -1% to -4% unless accompanied by strong cost guidance
- 1M: Varies widely; driven by subsequent earnings results and management commentary

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Stock was already oversold (-30%+ from highs) and layoffs signal efficiency pivot
- Company provides clear cost savings guidance ($Xm/year in savings) with layoff announcement
- Strong balance sheet; layoffs are strategic, not distressed
- Bull market environment where restructuring is rewarded

**Follow the reaction (negative) when:**
- Layoffs are in direct response to demand weakness ("not enough orders to fulfill")
- Company has declining revenue AND cutting headcount
- Sector-wide layoffs (signals industry-wide demand contraction)
- Post-2024 AI-related layoffs: 56% traded negative from announcement (data through May 2026)

### MERGER — TARGET COMPANY

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Gap up immediately to 80-95% of offer price (merger arb spread remains for deal uncertainty)
- 1D: +20% to +35% vs. pre-announcement unaffected price
- 1W: Trades at slight discount to offer (1-5% below); deal risk premium maintained
- 1M: Converges toward offer price if no regulatory issues; widened spread if regulatory concerns emerge

**Trading mechanics:**
- Cash deal announced at $50: stock immediately trades to ~$48-$49 (1-4% discount = arb spread)
- Stock deal: discount is larger (arb must hedge acquirer stock simultaneously)
- Never buy target above the announced offer price expecting a bump (occasionally happens but not reliable)
- Competitive bid scenario: can trade above first offer; second bidder historically bids +10-15% above first

---

### MERGER — ACQUIRER COMPANY

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: -1% to -3% at open
- 1D: -2% to -4%
- 1W: -2% to -5%
- 1M: -3% to -8% on average

**Fade the reaction (buy the acquirer dip) when:**
- Strategic acquisition in exactly right area (fills clear product gap)
- Acquisition price is low multiple vs. earnings; obvious synergies
- Bolt-on acquisition; minimal integration risk; no balance sheet strain
- Acquirer has proven M&A track record

**Follow the reaction (avoid or short) when:**
- Large transformative acquisition at high premium (>40% premium)
- Debt-financed deal that materially changes balance sheet quality
- Management's first large acquisition (execution risk high)
- Regulatory approval risk is significant (antitrust concerns)

### FED HAWKISH SURPRISE

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: -1% to -2% immediate; rate-sensitive sectors (tech, REITs) lead decline
- 1D: -1% to -3%; bond yields rise; dollar strengthens
- 1W: -2% to -4%; repricing continues as market adjusts rate expectations
- 1M: -3% to -6% in aggressive hawkish surprises (e.g., 75bps unexpected hike)

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Hawkish surprise was purely communication, not new policy information (verbal hawkishness only)
- Inflation data subsequently comes in below expectations (shows Fed will back off)
- Market had already priced in very aggressive tightening (consensus was already hawkish)

**Follow the reaction when:**
- Inflation clearly re-accelerating; Fed has credibility problem
- Fed signals explicit QT (quantitative tightening) acceleration on top of hikes
- Financial conditions significantly looser than Fed intentions (suggests more work to do)

---

### FED DOVISH SURPRISE

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: +1% to +2%; tech/growth leads; rate-sensitive sectors rally
- 1D: +1% to +2.5%
- 1W: +2% to +4%; bond yields fall; dollar weakens; growth stocks re-rate
- 1M: +3% to +6% if macroeconomic data supports the pivot

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Dovish only because economic data is terrible (pivot signals recession, not accommodation)
- Inflation still above target; market questioning Fed's credibility on inflation fight
- Currency markets signal concern (sharp dollar decline may signal stagflation fear)

**Follow the reaction when:**
- Inflation clearly defeated; pivot from strength not weakness
- Employment still strong; soft landing confirmed
- Classic "insurance cut" scenario; best possible macro environment for equities

### PRODUCT LAUNCH (APPLE, TESLA, MAJOR TECH)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: Flat to slightly negative at launch event; "sell the news" reaction common
- 1D: -1% to -3% for anticipated launches; flat to +2% for surprise launches
- 1W: -1% to -4% as speculation premium unwinds
- 1M: Returns to fundamental trajectory; product sales data begins to matter

**Fade the reaction (buy the sell-the-news dip) when:**
- Stock was up 10-20% in the 6 weeks before launch (speculation premium = expected fade)
- Fundamentals remain intact; product is solid but just "priced in"
- Seasonal buying typically resumes 2-4 weeks post-launch as actual demand data emerges

**Follow the reaction (hold or add) when:**
- Product is genuinely transformational (rare); moves an entirely new market category
- Pre-launch estimates were conservative; product beats on specs AND pricing AND availability
- Historical exception: iPhone (2007), M1 chip (2020) — buy and hold through the launch

### GEOPOLITICAL SHOCK

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: -1% to -3% immediate; safe havens (gold, bonds, yen) rally
- 1D: -3% to -6%; VIX spikes; energy stocks may rally if Middle East conflict
- 1W: -2% to -4% (partial recovery begins within days as situation assessed)
- 1M: Fully recovered in ~42 days historically; often positive vs. pre-shock price

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Geopolitical event does not create a lasting economic disruption
- Initial selloff > 5% without economic transmission mechanism (trade routes, commodity supply)
- Historical precedent: 19 of 20 major geopolitical shocks since WWII were buying opportunities within 3 weeks

**Follow the reaction (stay cautious) when:**
- Event is an energy shock (1973 style oil embargo); direct inflation transmission
- Involves nuclear escalation risk (market cannot price tail risk; uncertainty premium sustained)
- Event disrupts major supply chains for 6+ months
- Event involves a major economy directly (U.S., China, EU) — not peripheral conflict

**Sector playbook on geopolitical shock:**
- BUY: Defense stocks, Energy (oil spike), Gold miners
- AVOID: Airlines, Tourism, Consumer discretionary
- FADE (buy after initial selloff): Broad market S&P 500 ETFs (SPY/QQQ) historically recover fastest

### FDA APPROVAL (BIOTECH)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: +20% to +50% gap up immediately for small biotech; +2% to +5% for large pharma
- 1D: +30% to +60% average (small biotech); +3% to +8% (large pharma)
- 1W: +20% to +40% sustained (new revenue stream being priced)
- 1M: Depends on commercialization guidance and reimbursement outlook

**Fade the reaction when:**
- Approval was widely expected (90%+ market-implied probability from options pricing)
- Approved label is narrower than expected (limited patient population)
- Reimbursement/pricing risk remains (especially for rare disease drugs post-IRA)
- Stock was already up 50-100% in 6 months pre-approval pricing in the event

**Follow the reaction when:**
- Surprise approval (not widely expected)
- First-in-class drug with large addressable market
- Fast-track or breakthrough therapy designation (validates clinical significance)
- No competitor approvals in same class imminent

---

### FDA REJECTION (BIOTECH COMPLETE RESPONSE LETTER)

**Typical Reaction:**
- 1H: -40% to -70% immediate for single-product small biotech
- 1D: -50% to -75% average for small biotech; -5% to -10% for large pharma
- 1W: Further -5% to -15% as analysts remove drug from models
- 1M: May stabilize if company has other pipeline; catastrophic if single-asset

**Fade the reaction (cautious contrarian) when:**
- Rejection was for manufacturing/CMC issues (fixable); not clinical efficacy or safety
- Complete Response Letter specifies limited additional requirements
- Company has other pipeline assets not affected by this rejection
- Stock overreacted to 0% chance of approval when CRL issues were known

**Follow the reaction (avoid the name) when:**
- Clinical hold or safety rejection (not fixable; FDA rarely reverses on safety)
- Single-asset company; this was the only shot
- Management communication is vague or defensive about the path forward
