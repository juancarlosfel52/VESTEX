# Market Psychology — Contrarian Signal Guide
## When Extremes Historically Reversed | Thresholds | Actionable Interpretation

---

## THE CONTRARIAN PRINCIPLE
Market extremes in sentiment historically precede reversals. The crowd is right during trends but wrong at turning points. This guide focuses on QUANTIFIABLE thresholds, not narratives.

---

## FEAR & GREED INDEX — CONTRARIAN SIGNALS

### Extreme Fear (Index 0-24)
- **Historical reverse signal**: Within 30-90 days, markets tend to recover
- **Avg S&P 500 return 30 days after index < 20**: +4.2%
- **Avg S&P 500 return 90 days after index < 20**: +9.1%
- **Avg S&P 500 return 1 year after index < 20**: +17.4%
- **Caveat**: During sustained bear markets (2000-2002, 2008-2009), index stayed below 25 for months; timing remains challenging
- **Key historical instances**:
  - March 18, 2020: Index = 2 → S&P 500 +65% over next 12 months (COVID bottom)
  - October 27, 2022: Index = 11 → S&P 500 +25% over next 12 months
  - December 24, 2018: Index = 8 → S&P 500 +37% over next 12 months
  - August 24, 2015: Index = 6 → S&P 500 +14% over next 12 months
- **Signal reliability**: 75-80% of instances where index < 20 produced positive 6-month returns

### Extreme Greed (Index 75-100)
- **Historical reverse signal**: Returns drop significantly over next 6-12 months
- **Avg S&P 500 return 30 days after index > 80**: +0.3% (essentially flat)
- **Avg S&P 500 return 90 days after index > 80**: +1.1%
- **Avg S&P 500 return 1 year after index > 80**: +4.2% (well below historical 10% avg)
- **Key historical instances**:
  - November 2021: Index = 82 → S&P 500 -19% over next 12 months
  - January 2020 (pre-COVID): Index = 79 → COVID crash followed 6 weeks later
  - December 2019: Index = 92 (brief) → flat over next 2 months before crash
- **Signal reliability**: 65-70% of instances where index > 80 produced below-average 12-month returns
- **NOTE**: Extreme greed can persist in strong bull markets; use as a REDUCE / HEDGE signal, not a pure short signal

| < 12 | Extreme Calm | +0.6% | +3.8% | +8.4% |

| 20-30 | Elevated | +1.2% | +6.1% | +11.8% |

*Based on S&P 500 returns following VIX level crossings, 1990-2023*

### VIX Spike Reversal Timing
- VIX typically reverts to mean within 30-90 days after major spike
- Median days for VIX to fall from >40 back to below 25: 47 days (historical)
- Longest VIX elevation above 30: 2008-2009 crisis (sustained above 30 for 8 months)
- Rule: The higher the VIX spike, the more violent the eventual relief rally

### VIX-Based Trading Rules (Historical Backtests)
1. **VIX crosses above 30**: Statistical buy signal for S&P 500; wins 72% of the time over next 30 days
2. **VIX crosses above 40**: Statistical strong buy signal; wins 85% of the time over next 60 days
3. **VIX < 12 for 20+ consecutive days**: Start reducing risk; market historically complacent before volatility eruption
4. **VIX backwardation (near > far)**: Acute fear; typically resolves bullishly within 2-4 weeks

## PUT/CALL RATIO — CONTRARIAN SIGNALS

### Key Thresholds and Historical Returns

| Total P/C Ratio (5-day avg) | Sentiment Read | Avg 20-Day Forward S&P Return |
|-----------------------------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| > 1.20 | Extreme Fear | +3.1% |
| 1.00-1.20 | Fear | +1.9% |
| 0.80-1.00 | Neutral | +0.8% |
| 0.65-0.80 | Complacency | +0.3% |
| < 0.65 | Extreme Greed | -0.9% |

### Signal Confirmation Rule
- Strongest signals: P/C ratio extreme CONFIRMED by VIX at same extreme
- VIX > 40 AND P/C ratio > 1.2 simultaneously: avg 30-day return = +5.8%
- VIX < 15 AND P/C ratio < 0.65 simultaneously: avg 30-day return = -0.4%

### Zero-Day Options Distortion (2022-Present)
- 0DTE (zero days to expiration) options have grown to 50%+ of daily options volume by 2023
- These options expire same day and do not appear meaningfully in traditional P/C ratio calculations
- Post-2022: traditional P/C signals are somewhat less reliable; supplement with VIX and SKEW index

## CAPITULATION SIGNALS — BOTTOM IDENTIFICATION

### Multi-Factor Capitulation Checklist
When 4 or more of these fire simultaneously, the probability of a market bottom is high:

| Signal | Threshold | Frequency at Bottoms |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| VIX spike | > 40 | Present at 90% of major bottoms |
| P/C ratio 5-day avg | > 1.15 | Present at 80% of major bottoms |
| AAII Bears | > 55% | Present at 75% of major bottoms |
| NYSE New 52-wk lows | > 400 on single day | Present at 70% of major bottoms |
| 90% Down Day | NYSE down volume > 90% | Present at 65% of major bottoms |
| TRIN | Single-day > 2.5 | Present at 60% of major bottoms |
| Margin debt | 3-month decline > 10% | Present at 55% of major bottoms |
| Advance-Decline | Breadth thrust after extreme | Precedes 85% of recovery rallies |

### After Capitulation: Typical Recovery Pattern
1. **Day 1-5**: Violent snap-back rally (+5% to +10%); relief bounce
2. **Week 2-4**: Consolidation / sideways; some give-back (25-40% retracement of initial bounce)
3. **Month 2-3**: Re-test of lows on LESS volume (the key — less volume confirms buyers absorbing sellers)
4. **Month 3-6**: Confirmed uptrend begins; breadth expands
- Rule: The initial bounce from capitulation is REAL but the re-test is typically required for confirmation

### False Capitulation Warning
- Rally on high volume from "bottom" that fails and makes new lows = not true capitulation
- Multiple 90% down days required in severe bear markets (2008 had 8 separate 90% down days before the true bottom)
- True capitulation accompanied by: news being "terrible" (so everyone already knows), analyst downgrades accelerating, and retail investors giving up

## FOMO / PARABOLIC MOVES — REVERSAL SIGNALS

### Parabolic Move Characteristics and Average Reversal
- Stock up >100% in < 60 days with no fundamental change: average subsequent -60% retracement
- RSI sustained > 85 for 5+ days: Average subsequent reversion to RSI 40-50 within 30-45 days
- Volume 5x average during final parabolic phase: Classic distribution signal
- Notable historical reversals after parabolic FOMO:
  - Bitcoin Nov-Dec 2017: +100% in 30 days → -83% over next 12 months
  - GameStop Jan 2021: +1,700% in 3 weeks → -90% over next 6 months
  - Peloton Nov 2020: +140% in 60 days → -92% from peak to trough
  - Cathie Wood ARK Innovation (ARKK) 2020-2021: +350% → -75% from peak

### FOMO Reversal Timing
- First reversal from parabolic peak: Often within 5-15 days after volume climax
- Full mean reversion: Typically 3-12 months
- "Dead cat bounce" rule: First major bounce after parabolic peak retraces ~30-40% of the initial decline, then resumes lower
- Short squeezes specifically: Average 75-85% retracement from peak within 90 days

## BEAR TRAP — IDENTIFICATION AND RECOVERY

### Bear Trap Pattern
1. Market/stock breaks below major support (200-day MA, key price level, trend line)
2. Volume is lower than expected on the breakdown (lack of conviction)
3. Reversal happens quickly — within 1-3 days
4. Close above the broken support level
5. Short sellers trapped; their buying fuels the recovery rally

### Historical Bear Trap Examples
- October 11, 2022: S&P 500 broke below June 2022 low (~3,636) by 30 points intraday; reversed to close above → launched +14% rally over next 2 months
- January 2019: S&P 500 briefly broke below December 2018 lows on light volume → reversed sharply; +20% rally followed
- October 1987 aftermath: Multiple bear trap setups as market tried to retest crash lows

### Bear Trap Confirmation Rules
- Volume must be LOWER on the breakdown vs. the original move that established support
- Recovery above the broken level should happen within 3-5 days maximum
- Wide-market breadth improving rapidly after the reversal (AD line turning up)
- Average return after confirmed bear trap: +8% to +15% over next 20 trading days

## BULL TRAP — IDENTIFICATION AND AVOIDANCE

### Bull Trap Pattern
1. Market/stock breaks ABOVE major resistance (downtrend line, key high, moving average)
2. Buyers enter based on the breakout
3. Volume may look high (especially on first day)
4. Reversal happens within 3-7 days
5. Long buyers trapped; their selling accelerates the decline

### Historical Bull Trap Examples
- August 2022: S&P 500 rallied +17% from June lows, breaking above 50-day MA; looked like new bull market; reversed and fell -16% to new cycle lows
- January 2001 (post dot-com): Multiple 10-15% rallies that all failed; NASDAQ fell further after each
- March 2009 bear market: Multiple bear market rallies of 8-15% before the true March 9 bottom

### Bull Trap Warning Signs
- Breakout on LOW volume (not confirmed by participation)
- Breadth not improving alongside price breakout
- Still in declining earnings revision cycle (analyst estimates still being cut)
- Options market not confirming (IV not declining meaningfully)
- Average return after entering a bull trap: -12% to -20% over next 30 days

## BEHAVIORAL BIAS SIGNALS — MARKET TIMING IMPLICATIONS

### Loss Aversion in Practice
- After a 20%+ drawdown, retail investors are statistically MOST LIKELY to sell (maximum pain)
- This is typically the worst time to sell (capitulation = close to bottom)
- Contrarian rule: Periods of maximum retail selling (visible in fund flow data) historically near bottoms

### Anchoring Effect
- When S&P 500 or a stock trades near a major round number (3,000, 4,000, etc.) for extended time, that number becomes an anchor
- Breaks above major round number anchors (S&P 500 crossing 4,000 for first time): avg +5% additional gain over next 30 days (anchoring bulls buy the confirmation)
- Breaks below major round number anchors: accelerated selling as anchoring holders panic

### Recency Bias Signal
- DALBAR data: After 3-year bear markets, retail investors have the lowest equity allocation of the cycle → historically precedes strong recovery
- After 3+ years of bull market: Highest equity allocation → historically precedes underperformance
- Rule: When DALBAR surveys show investors shifted most money INTO equities, be cautious
